The Players of Dry Gulch
🔴
The Kung-Fu Master
The Wandering Fist — Hero Type A
Barefoot. Poncho over flowing red robes. Speaks seldom. Moves always. The bamboo flute slung across his back is both instrument and weapon — its harmonics can shatter steel valves, scatter enforcers, and apparently restructure aquifer geology. Probably. The alien compilers were not certain.
Bamboo Flute (Lethal)
🟡
The Samurai Cowboy
The Iron Drifter — Hero Type B
Arrives by pink-smoke train. Hat brim, katana, six-gun, spurred boots, and an obi belt over a duster coat. His code demands he protect the gathering. He is accompanied at all times by a troupe of Traveling Dancers who apparently function as tactical support in floral formation.
Katana + Six-Gun (Honor-Locked)
🔵
CEO Cactus
The Gold-Spined Monopolist — Principal Villain
Towers above lesser cacti. Gold-plated spines. Polished boots. Monocle. His speech balloons are shaped like profit charts. Commands a fleet of steam-powered pump contraptions. Has monetized the town's only water supply. Believes, sincerely, that he is the civilization.
Cactus-Cola Reserve (Weaponized Liquidity)
🦎
The Gila Bandits
Anthropomorphic Chaos — Secondary Threat
Bandana-wearing Gila monsters. Armed with rattlesnake rifles that fire coils of rope. They work the Glow-Rock Mine for the Corporate Cacti, though their loyalty is mercurial at best. When the tide turns, they tend to simply leave. One gets drop-kicked into a water trough. It's fine.
Rattlesnake Rifles (Coil Ammunition)
👩‍🏫
The Schoolmarm
The Quiet Spine of Dry Gulch
Stitches the Samurai's sleeve. Speaks plainly. Does not flinch. Her eye contact conveys more than most characters' full monologues. She represents the town's dignity — the reason the water matters, the reason any of this is worth the violence of song.
Needle & Thread (Emotionally Lethal)
💃
The Traveling Dancers
Kabuki-Can-Can Tactical Unit
Arrive with the Samurai. Kimono-and-feather hybrid costumes. Their can-can-kabuki fusion performances are also: ambush formations, morale operations, and genuine cutting threats (steel-lined fans). The lead dancer taps the Prospector's nose with a fan. This is considered the most romantic gesture in the series.
Steel-Lined Fans (Bladed / Performative)
Tone Refinement Notes — Alien Algorithm Publishing Directive #7741
The Laws of This
Ridiculous Universe
I
Villain Vocabulary Make villains talk in absurd corporate jargon at all times. "Liquidity." "Market correction." "Hydration as premium service." They are entirely sincere. This is the horror.
II
Hero Minimalism Keep heroes mostly wordless. When they do speak, it should land like a katana strike. "Water remembers its path." / "Then we help it choose." That's the whole thing.
III
The Silent Panel Every major strike must be preceded by a wide, wordless panel. One breath. One held moment. Then: chaos.
IV
Sound as Weapon Flute harmonics, taiko reverb, shamisen pluck — draw musical soundwaves as tangible, physical forces. They crack armor. They shatter valves. They are real.
V
The Collision Is the Point Never explain the genre collision. Treat flute acoustics, glow-rock physics, and cactus capitalism as self-evident truths. The weirdness is not the quirk — it is the foundation.
VI
The Mandatory Splash Every issue needs at least one explosive full-page spread. Something that could be hung on a wall. Something that makes a child point and say: "that one."

"The real secret sauce? Never explain too much. Lean into the weirdness until it sings. The alien compilers watched ten thousand hours of cinema and concluded: this was everything. They were not entirely wrong."
